GraphSpace is a tool for storing, sharing, and visualizing graphs in order to help collaborative research. A user of GraphSpace may upload graphs of any kind. In particular, one may represent biological interactions through the use of nodes and edges. For example, a node may be thought of as a protein. If there exists an edge between two nodes in the network(graph), this may indicate that the two proteins interact. Biological networks may contain complex interactions between many different proteins. As a result, we provide a tool for re-arranging the structure of any graph on GraphSpace by allowing the user to save their custom arrangement or layouts of these graphs. When a user uploads a graph to GraphSpace, they have the option of specifying whether the graph is to be public (viewable to all users/visitors on GraphSpace) or be private (viewable to only the creator of the graph). To aid collaboration, we define the concept of a group. A group consists of users who have permission to see private graphs. Any user of GraphSpace may create their own group and invite existing users of GraphSpace to join their group. Users may share any graph or layout of a graph which they own with members of a group.

It was developed by the Murali lab at Virginia Tech.
